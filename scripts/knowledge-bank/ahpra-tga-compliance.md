# AHPRA & TGA Compliance Knowledge Bank

## AHPRA Section 133 — Health Practitioner Advertising

### PROHIBITED in ALL marketing:
- Patient testimonials, reviews, or case studies with patient identifiers
- Guaranteed outcomes ("you will lose X kg", "guaranteed results")
- Superlatives without Level I/II evidence ("best", "leading", "most effective", "number one")
- Before/after photos without: informed consent, unedited, appropriate context, disclaimers
- Fear-based messaging or pressure tactics
- Specialist title without specialist registration
- "Dr" for non-medical practitioners (NPs are NOT doctors)
- Discounts, prizes, or inducements to attract patients
- Comparisons with other practitioners
- Claims that could create unreasonable expectations
- Endorsements by celebrities or public figures

### REQUIRED where relevant:
- Accurate practitioner titles matching AHPRA registration
- Risk information for any procedure/treatment mentioned
- "Individual results may vary" for outcome-related content
- Appropriate disclaimers on health information
- Date of content creation/update

### AI Content Accountability (Sep 2025)
- AHPRA now uses AI to scan websites and social media
- AI-generated content carries SAME legal liability as manually created
- The registered practitioner is accountable for ALL content
- Recommend: "Content reviewed by [Practitioner Name], [Registration Type]"

### Penalties
- Individual: up to $60,000 per offence
- Body corporate: up to $120,000 per offence
- AHPRA can: issue caution, impose conditions, suspend registration

## TGA Therapeutic Goods Advertising Code

### Schedule 4 (S4) — Prescription Only
- NEVER name S4 medications to consumers
- Includes: semaglutide, tirzepatide, liraglutide, tretinoin, adapalene, isotretinoin
- Can advertise the PRESCRIBING SERVICE, not the drug
- Can say "prescription weight loss medication" generically
- Can say "clinician-prescribed treatment"
- Can describe the consultation process

### TGA Category Rules by Industry

#### Weight Loss
- NEVER name: Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro, Saxenda, semaglutide, tirzepatide, liraglutide
- USE: "prescription weight loss medications", "evidence-based weight management"
- NEVER promise specific weight loss amounts
- USE: "work toward your personal weight goals"
- Must emphasise medication is part of comprehensive approach

#### Skincare/Dermatology
- NEVER name: tretinoin, adapalene, isotretinoin
- USE: "prescription-strength skincare", "medical-grade skincare"
- Cannot claim to "cure" acne, ageing, or skin conditions
- Can discuss evidence-based approaches to skin health

#### Cannabis
- STRICTLY PROHIBITED: advertising cannabis products to the public
- Cannot use terms like "plant medicine"
- Can educate about legal access pathways (SAS-B, AP)

### Safe Language Reference Table

| AVOID | USE INSTEAD |
|-------|-------------|
| "Guaranteed results" | "Evidence-based treatment" |
| "Best weight loss clinic" | "Affordable, accessible weight loss care" |
| "Lose X kg" | "Work toward your personal weight goals" |
| "Our [drug name] treatment" | "Our clinician-prescribed treatment" |
| "Dr [Name]" (for NPs) | "NP [Name]" or "Nurse Practitioner [Name]" |
| "Cure your [condition]" | "Manage your [condition]" |
| "100% safe" | "Evidence-based with a well-understood safety profile" |
| "No side effects" | "Side effects are discussed during your consultation" |
| "Before and after" (no context) | Include: "Individual results may vary. Unedited images." |
| "Number one / leading / best" | Remove or cite Level I/II evidence |
