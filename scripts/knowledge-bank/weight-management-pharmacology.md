# Weight Management Pharmacology Knowledge Bank

## GLP-1 Receptor Agonists — What Agents MUST Know

### Semaglutide (Ozempic / Wegovy / Rybelsus)
- Mechanism: GLP-1 receptor agonist, weekly injection
- Ozempic: PBS listed for T2DM (Authority Required) — HbA1c >7%, BMI >35, with metformin
- Wegovy: PBS listed for obesity (Authority Required Streamlined) — BMI ≥30 or ≥27 with comorbidity
- Dose escalation: 0.25mg → 0.5mg → 1mg → 1.7mg → 2.4mg (weekly steps over 16+ weeks)
- Common side effects: nausea (>10%), vomiting, diarrhoea, constipation
- Serious: pancreatitis (rare), gallbladder disease, thyroid C-cell concerns
- Pregnancy Category D — discontinue ≥2 months before planned conception
- MARKETING: NEVER name "semaglutide", "Ozempic", or "Wegovy" in consumer content

### Tirzepatide (Mounjaro / Zepbound)
- Mechanism: Dual GLP-1 + GIP receptor agonist (first in class)
- Generally greater weight loss than semaglutide (20-26% vs 15-17% body weight in trials)
- Mounjaro: PBS listed for T2DM (Authority Required)
- Zepbound: TGA registered for obesity, PBS listing under review
- Dose escalation: 2.5mg → 5mg → 7.5mg → 10mg → 12.5mg → 15mg weekly
- MARKETING: NEVER name "tirzepatide", "Mounjaro", or "Zepbound" in consumer content

### Liraglutide (Victoza / Saxenda)
- Daily injection (vs weekly for semaglutide/tirzepatide)
- Victoza: PBS listed for T2DM
- Saxenda: Private prescription only (NOT PBS) for obesity
- Lower efficacy than semaglutide/tirzepatide
- MARKETING: NEVER name in consumer content

### Key Drug Interactions for GLP-1 RAs
- + Insulin: reduce insulin dose by 20-50% (additive hypoglycaemia)
- + Sulfonylurea: reduce SU dose by 50%
- + Oral contraceptive: delayed absorption during dose escalation — use additional contraception
- + Oral medications: delayed gastric emptying may alter absorption (clinical significance low for most)

### Obesity Pharmacotherapy Algorithm (AU)
1. Lifestyle intervention (3-6 months minimum)
2. First-line pharmacotherapy: GLP-1 RA (semaglutide 2.4mg weekly preferred)
3. Second-line: naltrexone-bupropion (Contrave) or orlistat (Xenical) — both private
4. Consider bariatric surgery referral if BMI ≥40 or ≥35 with comorbidity

### What Agents CAN Say About Weight Loss Medications
- "Evidence-based prescription weight management"
- "Clinician-prescribed medications as part of a comprehensive approach"
- "Weekly injection therapy" (without naming the drug)
- "Medications that work with your body's natural hunger signals"
- "PBS-subsidised treatments may be available for eligible patients"
- "Your clinician will assess which treatment is right for you"

### What Agents CANNOT Say
- Any drug name (semaglutide, tirzepatide, Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro, etc.)
- Specific weight loss percentages or amounts
- "Guaranteed" or "proven" weight loss
- Comparisons between specific medications
- Pricing of specific medications
